About NITW & Mechanical Department

Rooted in Excellence

About NIT Warangal:
Established in 1959, the first in the chain of 31 NITs in the country. Recognized as an Institute of National Importance and ranked among the top technical education institutions in India. Features 14 Departments offering 10 undergraduate (B.Tech) and 29 postgraduate programs, plus research programs in engineering, sciences, humanities, physical education, and management. A residential campus with about 7000 students from across the country and approximately 500 international students.

About Mechanical Engineering Department (MED):
Established in 1959, since the Institute's inception. Actively engaged in teaching and research in diverse fields of Mechanical Engineering. Comprises 51 faculties holding Ph.D. from reputed Indian and foreign Universities. Offers 1 Undergraduate (B.Tech.) and 5 Postgraduate (M.Tech) courses. Has over 400 SCI/Scopus Indexed journals in the last 3 years.

Unveiling ICTSE 2026: A Convergence of Minds

About the Conference (ICTSE 2026)

Aims to provide a leading forum for researchers and engineers from academia and industry to exchange advancements and cutting-edge research in heat transfer and fluid dynamics. Strives to showcase and disseminate recent theoretical, computational, and experimental findings in heat transfer and fluid flow, covering fundamental principles and novel applications. Aims to foster new research partnerships, knowledge sharing, and technology transfer to address emerging challenges related to sustainable energy systems, energy efficiency, climate change mitigation and adaptation, and biomedical applications and healthcare.

Guidelines for Paper Submission

General Instructions

  • Papers must be original and not previously published
  • All papers should be written in English and follow IEEE format
  • Maximum paper length is 6 pages, including figures and references
  • All submissions will be peer-reviewed by experts in the field

Thermal Track

  • Focus on heat transfer and thermal systems
  • Include experimental/analytical results
  • Provide clear methodology
  • Highlight practical applications

Fluid Track
